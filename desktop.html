Hey there, HomeBaker!

It’s time for this month’s *Byte of the Month* — and we’re going full nostalgia:

Classic Fudgy Brownies — simple, gooey, and dangerously good.

Ingredients:
• 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted
• 1 cup granulated sugar
• 2 large eggs
• 1 tsp vanilla extract
• 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
• 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
• 1/4 tsp salt
• 1/4 tsp baking powder
• (Optional) 1/2 cup chocolate chips or chopped nuts

Directions:
1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease an 8x8" pan.
2. Mix butter & sugar. Add eggs + vanilla.
3. Stir in dry ingredients. Fold in any extras.
4. Bake 20–25 minutes (or until just set).
5. Cool. Slice. Pretend you’ll eat just one.


Until next time,

— The HomeByte Kitchen Crew

sometimes, i find it hard to say how i feel.

but flowers have a way of saying things i can’t quite put into words.

roses = timeless beauty and the complexity of human emotion. its bloom speaks to love and desire, while its thorns remind us that even beauty comes with challenges.

sunflowers = warmth. like someone who just makes everything better with their presence. whenever i look at these, i feel comfort and it makes my day brighter. these are reserved for people i truly care about.

lily of the valley = quiet love. soft, but strong. the kind of love that’s always there no matter how far you are from each other.

forget-me-nots = memory. the kind of love or friendship that never really leaves you.

lavender = calm. peacefulness. like when you finally feel safe after a long day. these signify devotion without expectation – offering love because you genuinely feel it, not because you need something back.

peonies = hope. they remind me that even when things are messy, beauty can still grow.

lilies = a gentle way to say “i see you, i value you, and i’m thankful for the love and calm you bring into my life". they symbolize a quiet, steady kind of affection—one rooted in trust, gratitude, and emotional warmth. these lilies honor the beauty of gentle love, the kind that nurtures without needing to be loud.

baby’s breath = subtle love. not loud, but always there, quietly supporting.

amaryllis = standing out with a mix of strength and mystery. it’s a way to say you really admire someone, not just for how they look, but for their confidence and the deeper, more meaningful stuff about them. giving someone a purple amaryllis is like telling them, “You’re amazing, and I see the real you". it’s a flower that shows respect and a special kind of love that goes beyond just the surface.

tulips = new beginnings. fresh starts, even after things have ended.

orchids = admiration. something rare, strong, and beautiful that you can’t help but respect and adore.

For now, the manuscript’s true message remains a mystery.
